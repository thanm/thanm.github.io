Day 38: Holyoke CO to Sterling CO

May 27, 2025

Another fairly easy ride, 53 miles, 1237 elevation gain, and yet again we had reasonable favorable winds. We were following route 6 again the connecting to route 14, one lane each way, wide shoulder the whole way, decent surface, but the traffic was definitely heavy, with plenty of trucks.

It was interesting to watch the terrain change during this ride – at the start in the morning it was corn and wheat fields, surroundings virtually identical to what we were seeing in Nebraska – then at a certain point things start to change, and by the time you hit Sterling it is a completely different look, scrubby vegetation, very mildly rolling hills, ranchland, much drier (more like what I would expect to see around the Denver area.

Day 31: Washington KS to Mankato KS

May 20, 2025

Incredibly challenging ride today, one of the hardest rides I can recall doing.

The cue sheet was dead simple, just get on state route 36 for 63 miles, but the whole stretch had 20-30 mph headwinds with gusts that were stronger, so we had to dig deep for every mile, we really had our rear ends kicked up down and sideways.

I had to pedal hard just to make 10 or 11 mph; on the hills we were down to 6 or 7 mph (total elevation gain 2567). Six and a half hours of riding time, average speed of 10.5 mph. The road was decent overall, most sections had pretty good shoulders and the surface was quite good; traffic was modest except for the last couple of hours.
