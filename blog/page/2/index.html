Day 46: Rawlins WY to Jeffrey City WY

Jun 4, 2025

Long ride today, 69 miles, about 2000 feet of elevation gain, and some modest headwinds (7-10 mph nothing too intense, but we had them to contend with for the last forty miles or so).

Eastern sky in Rawlins, before the sunrise:

We crossed the continental divide today, somewhat puzzling since the spot where it happened seemed rather undistinguished (just a little hill/ridge).

Lovely scenery on this ride, with lots of interesting changes in the landscape and the geology as the ride progressed. Instead of starting sunny and then clouding up later in the day (the pattern for our last handful of rides) it did the opposite, so when we finally rolled into our destination the weather was almost perfect (high 60’s, sunny, light breeze).

Day 44: Walden Colorado to Saratoga Wyoming

Jun 2, 2025

Today we rode from Walden Colorado to Saratoga Wyoming, so a new state, huzzah! A total of 69 miles, 2,542 feet of elevation gain – a fairly challenging ride by the numbers.

The landscape changed a bit as we rode north, maybe a bit drier and vegetation more scrubby than the North Park area we were in before.

Weather-wise this was a “Dr Jeckyl and Mr Hyde” ride – first 30 miles (kindly Dr Jeckyl) we had a modest tailwind, sailing along with ease. Then lo and behold around the 30 mile mark the wind turned around and we were riding into a 7-10mph headwind (horrible Mr Hyde). Lots of work from then on to keep up a reasonable pace. Legs pretty sore when we were done…
