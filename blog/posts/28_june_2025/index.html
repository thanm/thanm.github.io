Day 70: Prineville OR to Sisters OR

Posted on Jun 28, 2025

Another medium ride today, 51 miles, but with a minimum of climbing, 1795 feet elevation gain, 3 hours 41 minutes riding time. It was good to take it easy again.

I had a hard time getting to sleep the night before, there was to much loud traffic from the rodeo (basically people gunning their very large truck engines), it was a while until I fell asleep, and I was a bit late getting up (5:45am).

For the ride it was a slight downhill out of Prineville, no wind, easy spinning. The path followed a big rocky outcrop, winds around in the valley and eventually dumps out into a river valley. Then a climb up into a nice alpine plateau with really amazing views of the Sisters (mountains) off to the west – very beautiful.

Ride:

I rode with Chet, Tom, and Terence today, it was fun to hang with them and chat on the ride.

Stopped for coffee at Red Point in Terrebonne, they had really good coffee and a nice space for hanging out. Red Point sells rock climbing gear, then doubles as a coffee shop, then triples as a brewpub (bunch of local beers on tap). Seems like they have all the bases covered…

Later we dropped in on the store at Rainshadow Organics (a local greens farm) for snacks and water, they were perfectly positioned for us to refill.

Finally I had one more stop in Sisters as well; nice meal of tacos at a food truck on the main drag.
