Day 74: Final Ride: Alsea OR to Florence OR

Posted on Jul 2, 2025

Final ride today on the tour! It was a 66 miler with a wheel dip in the Pacific around mile 35 or so, just south of Waldport, total riding time a bit short of five hours. Plenty of elevation gain (3000+), all of it in the form of up-and-down small hills (just a ton of them). Terrain reminiscent of our riding in eastern Ohio.

The first section (30 miles or so) was following the Alsea river down to the coast, a winding two-lane road with not the greatest shoulder, but nice trees and wildlife. Got a good look at a pair of big red-headed woodpeckers a bit before mile 10 or so; riding with Terence and Tom for that section.

The ride as recorded by my phone – this was another one where some sort of strange software glitch added a bunch of phantom/nonexistent hills + miles to my real ride: all the strange jaggedy stuff from “mile 6” to “mile 35” is not really there:

Mouth of the Alsea river near Waldport:

NB: Not super impressed with Oregon drivers on this ride, my compadres and I were buzzed (close calls drive-by’s) more than once, mostly by people in big pickup trucks, and in situations where there wasn’t any oncoming traffic either. Can’t really explain it – maybe these folks were bitten on the leg by a rabid cyclist when they were little? Or maybe had some other bad experience with a bike that left them with unresolved anger issues? Who knows.

At Waldport we met up at a food store, made sure everyone was there, then rode together down to a beach campground about 4 miles south and stopped for our official “wheel dip”. Photos:

Views of the Pacific from the ride – very beautiful stretch of coastline along here, quite lovely indeed.

I rode with Chet’s triangle today as well as my own – he and Mel were with me on the ride in spirit, I was thinking of them.

Tonight we have a group dinner planned out, which I am looking forward to (camp cooking now pretty old), then one more night in the tent, and a van ride back to Eugene tomorrow.

Hard to believe we finally made it – it has been such a long haul getting here… an amazing experience in so many ways.
