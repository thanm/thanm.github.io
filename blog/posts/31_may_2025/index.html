Day 42: Fort Collins CO to Poudre River Canyon near Rustic CO

Posted on May 31, 2025

Fabulous ride today, really one of the nicest ones yet on the tour if not the best.

Perfect weather, sunny, warm sunshine with cool breezes, decent road conditions (no trucks) but most importantly fabulous scenery. 44 miles or thereabouts, a bit less than 3000 feet of climbing. First part (about 6 miles) was on a bike path leading out of the city, then eventually we switched to SR 14, a two-laner that wound its way up into the mountains.

Route 14 followed the Cache La Poudre river, a good-sized mountain river, clearly very full from snowmelt, water moving at a decent clip. On the way we had the river on one side, canyon wall on the other, lots of nice rock formations, tons of flowers and hummingbirds (I could hear them the whole way). Just gorgeous stuff.

We did have some climbing but it was fairly gradual, and I could feel some effects from the altitude but nothing too punishing. Nice stop around the 20ish mark at a food+coffee truck to have a breakfast burrito, then another small stop in the town of Rustic just a couple miles before the end of the road.

The campground looks good, small private RV park with decent gathering places and good showers.
