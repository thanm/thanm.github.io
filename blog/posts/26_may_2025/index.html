Day 37: Imperial NE to Holyoke CO

Posted on May 26, 2025

New state; Colorado, huzzah! Extra short ride today, 38 miles, so the cooks in charge of breakfast decided to do a really nice one using the church kitchen: bacon, eggs, and pancakes with maple syrup, yum. When we finally finished all the cleanup, packing, etc it was around 8:30 but still raining lightly… we sat around for a while trying to decide whether it was going to stop, but eventually just headed out into the wet after all. Not a big deal, it wasn’t a soaking rain, just little bitty misty drops. Riding was good, road a bit rough but there was a decent shoulder most of the way, and the traffic wasn’t all that bad.

Lot of grumbling from the gang when we rolled in about how cold the ride had been, and how they couldn’t seem to get warm… wasn’t an issue at all for me, I did just fine. I think all those years of biking to work in MA during the winter months appear to have paid off I guess.

We encountered our first west-to-east Transam rider this morning, a bearded older man doing it self-supported, so all loaded up with racks + panniers + bags. Nice guy, he said that he and his brother had started in Oregon and ridden together for a while, then the brother peeled off and decided to take the Northern Tier route, while he headed south towards the Katy trail in Missouri.

You’ll note from the elevation profile of this ride that we did another slow steady climb again, inching our way up to the 4900 feet we’ll be hitting in Fort Collins. High-altitude biking here we come…
