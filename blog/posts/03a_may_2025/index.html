Day 14: Down to ten riders

Posted on May 3, 2025

We lost another rider today, Scott decided to leave the tour; Dennis drove him to the airport, he is going to fly back home (we will figure out what to do with his bike at some later point in time, it is currently stashed on top of the van).

Bummed that we will not have him around, I enjoyed his sense of humor and his perspective as a seasoned California rider. He told us later that things had been going OK up until the first days in Ohio where we really started to do the serious hill climbing – in spite of trying his best, he was absolutely running out of gas on the climbs, and it was clear that something was really not right physically and that it was not just a normal “bike ride is tiring me out” type situation.

[NB: we heard much later in the tour from Simona that Scott turned out to have a bunch of serious undiagnosed problems with the veins/arteries in his legs, which was what was causing the issues once the really steep climbing began. His doctors were apparently flabbergasted that he had been able to make it as far as he did on the tour, which I think speaks to his level of commitment and effort up until we hit Ohio. Still sorry that things didn’t work out for him.]
