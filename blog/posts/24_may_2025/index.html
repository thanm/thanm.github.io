Day 35: Zero Day in McCook NE

Posted on May 24, 2025

No mileage today, we are relaxing here in McCook, Nebraska in a motel. It is a pleasure to sleep in a bed and have regular meals cooked by someone else …

This morning a group of wandered down to the center of town and saw the sights: a house designed by Frank Lloyd Wright, and a couple of museums, including The Museum of the High Plains, and a newer spot called the Art Bank.

A few photos:

Main street in McCook, the older part of town:

The Frank Lloyd Wright house here in town:

Plaque outlining the life and works of George Norris, an important historical figure for Nebraska:

Warm memorial statue in the city park. Nearly all of the towns we’ve passed through have warm memorials, even the smaller ones.

The Museum of the Plains was interesting, also very hodge-podge (no organizational scheme that I could determine whatsoever).

The nice lady running the show there got our her keys and let us into the railway caboose they have parked at the museum; we clambered around inside and got to see it up close. Not a very luxurious interior, it was mainly bunks where the train crew could get a bit of shut-eye from time to time.

Couple of pictures from the Art Bank. They had an exhibition in which an artist did a series of paintings about the massacre at Wounded Knee, which was quite something (very dark).

Dinner tonight at a slightly tonier restaurant that Chet picked out, looking forward to that.

From the forecast it looks like we will get rained on during our ride tomorrow, not giant amounts but we’ll need to gear up. Winds still favorable, 10-15 mph east-north-east, so that will be a blessing if the prediction holds.
