A first posting on my trans-am blog

Posted on Apr 15, 2025

Hi all, welcome to the blog I’m keeping to document the Trans-America bike ride I’m doing this summer. Although I’ve created a lot of content using Blogger/Blogspot, I decided this time around to “roll my own” so as to have a bit more control (you can click on the “about” link above for more of the grubby details).

A photo of my trusty bike:

This is a Salsa Cutthroat, a bike in theory designed for long-distance gravel rides (in particular the Tour Divide route). Carbon fiber frame to keep the weight down, but in other respects a high level of sturdiness for dealing with rough roads and such.

I’m going to try to capture each ride (the GPS track) and upload it so that you can see where I’ve been. Here’s an example training ride I did here in Arlington:

The data for the page above was generated by a Garmin Edge 130 (very minimalista GPS-enabled bike computer) and then uploaded to Ride With GPS. It’s a nice way to summarize a ride – shows the route as well as the elevation (and shows in embarassing detail just how slowly you had to ride in order to get over that big hill, heh).

This tour is sponsored/run by the Adventure Cycling Association, here is a link that has more details on the ride itself (route and such).

A heads-up that I won’t be trying to post every day, I don’t think I will have the bandwidth for that – also, a sizeable fraction of the campsites we’re staying at don’t have WIFI (some of them probably won’t even have cell reception). Also: although my new blogging platform has many desirable qualities, adding photos is a bit tricky (requires a lot of manual steps) so I may need to be a bit more stingy with pictures for a bit until I iron that out.

Very excited to begin my ride, stay tuned for more posts once things get kicked off…
