Day 57: Zero Mileage Day in Missoula Montana

Posted on Jun 16, 2025

Zero day in Missoula, really good to rest my legs and get recharged/reorganized. The usual stuff: laundry, repacking, working on my bike, stretching, napping. I also went out and bought a new bike lock (my old lock is still fine, but I somehow lost the key to it that I brought).

Also decided to hedge my bets a little on my tires, I was able to find a bike shop that had the exact model I am running, so I bought a single tire as a spare. Photo:

I can’t say enough how happy I am with these tires, I should add. They are big and solid enough to handle all sorts of horrendous bumps and debris, but they do well on the pavement as well. Can’t quite believe it, but I still have yet to get a flat (probably mostly luck, but I think the tires have something to do with it too).

Mel and Chet and I went to a veggie/vegan place for lunch today, I ordered a really superb Brussels Caesar salad (photo). Great to have a nice big bown of greens for a change instead of a small portion.

I did at least a minimal amount of wandering around in the town to see what things looked like. They have a nice riverside park with a carousel in it, I was happy to see that:

Mural encountered on one of our walks:

Still a bit under the weather with a cold, but hopefully it will have blown over by tomorrow. I am making dinner tomorrow night with Terry, so we’ll need to do the shopping this evening (our destination, Powell Idaho, is microscopic and doesn’t have any sort of food store).
