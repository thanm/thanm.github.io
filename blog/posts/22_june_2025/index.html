Day 64: Cambridge ID to Halfway OR

Posted on Jun 22, 2025

This was an interesting “up and down and up” ride – lengthy climb up to the top of a pass, then descent down into Hell’s Canyon and along the Snake river, and then another climb getting back up out of the canyon and into the high plains again. Around 60 miles with a chunky 3600 feet of elevation gain. Weather behaved itself for the most part. Stiff headwind for the first 17 or 18 miles of the climb (ugh), but then wind was not really a factor for the remainder. I rode with Mike and Chet on this one.

It was threatening rain the whole way from mile 45 or so to mile 60 (coming up out of the canyon), but it never really amounted to much. A couple of times we stopped and put on all our rain gear… then 10 minutes later we had to stop and take it off again (getting too hot with it on).

Ride:

The Hell’s Canyon part was very scenic but kind of stark, there is only a little in the way of green growing stuff down in the canyon. Lots of people towing boats around on their cars getting ready to put in or having just taken out.

At mile 32 we crossed over the Snake river and offiically arrived in our final new state: Oregon … huzzah! Feels weird to not have any others on deck.

The Halfway Motel and RV park (pretty much the only option in town) is pretty basic, no wifi and a bit small / primitive. On the plus side the shower was nice and hot. Coupla photos (the RV Park sign was a classic, looks right out of the 1950s):
