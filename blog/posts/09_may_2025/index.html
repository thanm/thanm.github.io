Day 20: Casey IL to Vandalia IL

Posted on May 9, 2025

Lots more riding on SR 40, so we had to ride with care again and watch the traffic carefully. Good riding conditions though, no headwinds, sunny, weather really quite nice altogether. I rode with Mike for the most part, so pretty zippy speeds.

In Teutopolis IL we spotted a bunch of tents set up outside the Teutopolis Savings Bank, and discovered that they (the TSB employees) were having a charity cookout. We contributed some cash and sat down to have a burger, which was nice.

Coffee at Joe Sipper’s in Effingham, a very pleasant establishment. Stopped again in Altamont at the Charles M Wright house, good spot to sit for a bit and eat lunch.

With some tailwinds we pulled in on the early side at the Okaw Valley Campground outside of Vandalia, a very nice private CG establishment, enjoyed staying there. There was a bit of waiting around at that point – normally our van with its trailer is there at the campground when we show up, but for some reason Simona and Dennis were tied up and didn’t roll in until later.

It was my night to cook (with Terry), we eventually made out way with Simona to a nearbyhb Walmart and shopped for groceries. I think an integral part of the cooking experience on these ACA tours is being in a big grocery store in some place you’ve never heard of, running around trying to track down the ingredients you need (definitely describes our experience that afternoon).

Terry and I made German potato salad (which was great), sauteed mustard greens, some veggie dumplings for our two vegetarians, fruit salad, then we retooled/repuposed a bunch of leftovers from the night before (we had a bit too much food at that meal). Overall a nice meal.
