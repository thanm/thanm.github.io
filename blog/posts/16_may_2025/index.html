Day 27: Lawson MO to Atchison KS

Posted on May 16, 2025

Today’s ride was another really challenging one, on paper even more difficult than the previous day’s killer, but to be honest I found it a tiny bit easier, perhaps because it was more predictable (no chaos/mayhem introduced by routing around a bridge closure).

The first 20 miles or so featured nice scenery albeit with plenthy of hills, our route took us up to and around a big man-made lake near the town of Smithville. We stopped for a snack in the town of Paradise MO:

I rode with Mike today for the entire time, it was a good combo. From Smithville to Platte City a little wind and tons of hills – basically you would finish working yuour way up to the top of one hill, then at the top you could see a big dip and then the top of the next hill waiting for you in the distance. Lunch in Platte City outside the county courthouse.

A shot of hills appearing in the distance:

The ride map:

From Platte City we rode due northwest, and at this point the winds really started to pick up, so we had strong, gusting cross-winds and partial headwinds for a long stretch (maybe the next 25 miles), then for the last 5 or so due west into Atchison we were fighting our way through what seemed like gale-force headwinds (in reality more like 15-20 mph baseline with gusts up to 25-30), a real kick in the teeth. Whew!

Photo entering Kansas. This is just after we finished our killer headwind stretch, and before crossing a big bridge over the Missouri river:

We are staying at Warnock Lake Park and Camground, which has a bit of a state park feel but is operated by the city I think. Lovely spot and nice scenery, but pretty disgusting bathrooms with uncooperative lights (showers are not great either).

Super super windy, we didn’t even try to pitch our tents until the wind died down a bit after dinner. Here’s a photo of Mel taking shelter inside our trailer so as to be able to have a phone conversation:

No matter, since it is a zero day for us tomorrow, our first in a couple of weeks (13 days straight riding back to back). Yay!

Arlen and I cooked dinner this evening, we did a sort of make-your-own burrito thing (menu inspired by Arlen). I wound up doing probably a tiny bit more than my share since Arlen was tied up driving people to and from bike shops to deal with various broken bike issues.
