Day 67: John Day OR to Mitchell OR

Posted on Jun 26, 2025

Another fairly challenging day, 69 miles, 2800 feet of elevation gain, 5 hours 20 minutes ride time, but (at least for me) a good deal easier than yesterday.

Ride:

I rode with Theo for the first 40 miles or so, that was nice. We chatted about various stuff, I asked him about the Elfstedentocht, the big ice skating race in the Netherlands (which was last held in 1997) so we talked about that for a while and chewed over various other subjects.

At mile 40 the climbing started, also the headwinds; I wanted to ride ahead so I sped up a bit and rode by myself for the remainder. Very stark, dry landscape – from around mile 50 on I was looking for a place to stop on the side of the road with some shade, but there was basically nothing until pretty close to the pass.

Our destination here in Mitchell is the Spoke-n-Wheel bike hostel, which looks like a real winner in every way – nice beds, friendly folks taking care of it, good showers, awesome gathering area, good wifi… yay. Very happy to stay here.

Riding with Theo:

The start of the uphill was a section in which the road wound through a steep canyon called Picture Gorge – that was very beautiful…

There was a potential detour we could have made to the fossil museum (highly rated), but I was focused on finishing before the headwinds really kicked in, so I gave this a pass. Maybe on some other trip to Oregon:

Summit of the pass – all downhill from here…

The Spoke-n-Wheel:

Simona and Terry are cooking tonight – lucky them, they get to use the nice kitchen in the basement of the hostel. We’ll see what they come up with …

We’re winding down on the cycling days, we only have six (six!!!!) more riding days left. The end of the trip is coming up more quickly than I thought.

It feels good to knock off the last few big climbs as well; we have a big one tomorrow, then after that there’s mostly the smaller ups and downs and bumps. The final ride to Florence atually has a fair amount of elevation gain, but all all the ups are tiny (no monster 10 mile things to contend with).
