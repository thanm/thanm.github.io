Day 26: Higginsville MO to Watkins Mill State Park in Lawson MO

Posted on May 15, 2025

Super challenging ride today, 3000+ elevation gain, 68 miles, a substantial chunk with major headwinds. Not a lot of beautiful scenery or wildlife, just basically a ride to get from point A to point B.

We crossed over the Missouri again, that was a crazy section – very strong crosswind with major gusts, so all of us were trying very hard to stay on the shoulder and not get too close to the (small) guardrail.

The other main event with today’s ride was that we had to detour around a bridge that was out (they were building a new one), and we didn’t discover the outage until fairly late, so we had to tack on additional mileage in order to get around it (some folks more than others).

The state park we’re camping in looks great, not too busy, sites ok, good showers. The one weird thing was that the campsite itself was a fairly longish ride from the park entrance – with hills, gah.

Mel and Terry made an excellent meal this evening, fried fish, new potatoes, greeen beans, rolls, and cornbread – yum.
