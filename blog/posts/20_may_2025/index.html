Day 31: Washington KS to Mankato KS

Posted on May 20, 2025

Incredibly challenging ride today, one of the hardest rides I can recall doing.

The cue sheet was dead simple, just get on state route 36 for 63 miles, but the whole stretch had 20-30 mph headwinds with gusts that were stronger, so we had to dig deep for every mile, we really had our rear ends kicked up down and sideways.

I had to pedal hard just to make 10 or 11 mph; on the hills we were down to 6 or 7 mph (total elevation gain 2567). Six and a half hours of riding time, average speed of 10.5 mph. The road was decent overall, most sections had pretty good shoulders and the surface was quite good; traffic was modest except for the last couple of hours.

There were some really beautiful vistas along the way, one in particular where we were up on a rise and surrounded by green hay fields and by windmills in every direction as far as the eye could see.

The stops were nice – we peeled off from the route in Belleville and visited a coffee place (“The Feathered Nest”) with good java and friendly folks, then in Courtland KS we stopped at “The Depot Market”, which had nice produce (including fresh locally grown asparagus, Mike bought three bunches) and ice cream.

Asparagus picked up along the way:

Dinner cooked by Terry and Simona – fabulous meal, lots of nice veggies including the asparagus that we picked up at the local farmer’s market.

Our campground was visited by a very friendly and social cat – young, male, not neutered, super social, investigating everyone and everything. No collar, maybe a stray? But very human-centric for a stray. He tried with a whole bunch of us to invade our tents around bedtime (looking for a place to sleep I assume), but I shooed him off pronto, and I think everyone else did the same (inflatable sleeping pads not compatible with cat claws thank you very much).
