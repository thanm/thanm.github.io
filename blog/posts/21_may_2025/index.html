Day 32: Mankato KS to Franklin NE

Posted on May 21, 2025

New state today! We crossed over into Nebraska about a third of the way through our ride, which was to the west and north. The terrain and vegetation is noticeably different already – drier, more hay and less corn in the fields (and the corn we see is not as far along), more scrubby trees and bushes, sandier soil.

Much much easier ride than yesterday – around the same on paper (61 miles, 2500 feet of elevation) but mild headwinds (7-10mph) and as opposed to the gale force stuff we had on our previous ride. I rode with Chet and Mel the whole way – we had some hijinks, mainly a flat on Chet’s rear wheel that proved tricky to fix (we tackled it as a team).

Crossing into Nebraska:

We rode through the town of Red Cloud, the childhood home of Willa Cather, that was interesting. Brought back good memories of her books (“My Antonia”, “Death Comes to the Archbishop”).

Camping at yet another city park, but this one in Franklin seems like another nice one.

It was my night to cook, along with Mel. We made quesadillas for an appetizer, then pasta puttanesca, broccoli, salad, bacon bits. It was well received.

Looking forward to our rest day on Saturday in McCook NE …
