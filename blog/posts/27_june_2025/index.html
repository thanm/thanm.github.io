Day 69: Mitchell OR to Prineville OR

Posted on Jun 27, 2025

Medium ride today, 48 miles, but with 2600 feet of elevation gain in the form of a huge long climb right off the bat. The climb was not too bad, I was able to power up most of it; the remainder of the ride had headwinds but since it was a slight downhill it was not a punishing ride by any stretch of the imagination.

Ride:

We had very early start, since we we were staying at a hostel – no need to take time setting up camp kitchen or taking down tents, etc. At breakfast we all chatted with Dana one of the hostel caretakers; turns out she is a long distance hiker and had all sorts of stores about the PCT, the AZT, and the long trail.

Not too many photos today, I was just focused on getting the ride done and in the books. Also there wasn’t really much to see on the ride, not a lot of interesting stopping places. This was a ride to get from point A to point B, not to be admiring the stuff in between.

Partway up the climb I looked over to the side of the road and saw a cordless drill of all things perched on the guardrail – this has Tim’s fingerprints all over it of course. He likes to look for odd “stuff” that people have discarded on the side of the road (or in this case, fell off someone’s truck) … if the items are small enough he packs them along, otherwise he cues them up for us to admire:
