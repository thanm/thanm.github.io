Day 6: GAP trail ride from Cumberland MD to Confluence PA

Posted on Apr 25, 2025

Nice weather to start the day – I had a big of trouble sleeping the night before, but my head was clear and I was ready to ride by the time the 8am start time arrived.

On the plus side, the ride starting in Cumberland was fantastic – first day on the GAP (Great Allegheny Passage) trail, which follows a defunct rail line. First 25 miles or so uphill at a slight grade (2%) and then a long descend (1% grade down) into Confluence. That long downhill was delightful, really nice conditions.

Along the way we stopped at the Mason-Dixon line (I learned a few things about those two guys) and at the continental divide. We stuck together more as a group this time around, that was nice as well. Couple of interesting tunnels, including the Big Savage (biggest one we have been through yet) and some nice bridges.

Confluence is not a big town, but there was a decent campground with plenty of room to spread out. The camp was right at the base of a dam, with a very small hydro power installation beside it (one of the smallest I have seen). Also a very active trout fishing spot – apparently the water coming out the dam is very cold, making for some happy trout.

Bit of rain towards the end of the ride, just misty stuff however no need for real raingear. Nice dinner cooked by Terence and Mike.
