Day 38: Holyoke CO to Sterling CO

Posted on May 27, 2025

Another fairly easy ride, 53 miles, 1237 elevation gain, and yet again we had reasonable favorable winds. We were following route 6 again the connecting to route 14, one lane each way, wide shoulder the whole way, decent surface, but the traffic was definitely heavy, with plenty of trucks.

It was interesting to watch the terrain change during this ride – at the start in the morning it was corn and wheat fields, surroundings virtually identical to what we were seeing in Nebraska – then at a certain point things start to change, and by the time you hit Sterling it is a completely different look, scrubby vegetation, very mildly rolling hills, ranchland, much drier (more like what I would expect to see around the Denver area.

We stopped in Haxtun CO and discovered a new coffee place, “Phi Cappa Chino”, that was an enjoyable interlude at mile 18. Haxtun is about the same size as many of the small towns we passed in Nebraska, but it seemed to have a lot more vitality, didn’t have that “ghost town” atmosphere to it. At Fleming there weren’t any services to speak of, but our group stopped at a city park and had lunch.

Also in Haxtun was this mysterious sign near the grain elevator. What do these numbers mean – is this the price to sell corn? Buy corn? Is it per bushel? Per kilo? Lots of puzzles here. I am sure the locals know exactly how to interpret it.

New terrain on the road to Sterling:

Also interesting on this ride: I definitely noticed the altitude in a couple of sections when I was hill climbing or trying to overtake someone – even at a wimpy 4500 feet you can tell. More of that coming as we get into the real hills.

City park campground in Sterling:

Weirdly, we had not one but two locals come by and issue dire warnings to us about sleeping in the park – first a lady who told us that “tornados are on the way, the weather is going to get really dangerous” (?!?), then guy later on who helpfully offered “You should be aware that the crackheads and drug addicts will come by around midnight and steal anything that isn’t locked down” (?!?!). Our stay was uneventful however, we had neither tornados (weather was fine) nor visits from crackheads.

Tom and Theo cooking tonight; they are making some sort of Dutch dish that Theo picked out.

Sore back for some reason this afternoon, not sure why (since it was not exactly a heroic ride) – I will have to spend more time stretching tonight.
