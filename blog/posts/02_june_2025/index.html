Day 44: Walden Colorado to Saratoga Wyoming

Posted on Jun 2, 2025

Today we rode from Walden Colorado to Saratoga Wyoming, so a new state, huzzah! A total of 69 miles, 2,542 feet of elevation gain – a fairly challenging ride by the numbers.

The landscape changed a bit as we rode north, maybe a bit drier and vegetation more scrubby than the North Park area we were in before.

Weather-wise this was a “Dr Jeckyl and Mr Hyde” ride – first 30 miles (kindly Dr Jeckyl) we had a modest tailwind, sailing along with ease. Then lo and behold around the 30 mile mark the wind turned around and we were riding into a 7-10mph headwind (horrible Mr Hyde). Lots of work from then on to keep up a reasonable pace. Legs pretty sore when we were done…

Just as with our ride yesterday it started to cloud up around 11, and by 1pm or so there were intermittent bits of rain, but this time not a whole lot, we only had a couple of drops along the way.

Ride map:

If you look west from Walden on the map above you’ll see that the mountains directly to the west are the Zirkel range; Amy and I did a family hiking trip in this part of the Rockies back in the 90s; I remember it as a very wild, remote wilderness. We hiked the entire time there without seeing another soul, as I recall (which makes sense, given how long it takes to get there from Denver).

Instead of a campground, our destination for this ride was the Saint Barnabus Episcopalian church. They stashed us in one of the church outbuildings, a house with enough bedrooms to hold our gang (just barely) and a kitchen + dining area below. Very nice digs, super happy that they were willing to put us up.

Once we had settled in, a bunch of us went out to visit the local hot springs pool, about a half mile away on the banks of the river.

Couple of good-sized concrete bathing pools fed by hot springs, water was very hot. One of the folks I chatted with there said that it was around 111 degrees F, which is almost scalding. I stayed in for 5 or 6 minutes, then walked over to the river (the North Platte) and took a nice cold plunge, then repeated the process.
