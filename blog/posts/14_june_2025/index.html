Day 56: Wisdom MT to Darby MT

Posted on Jun 14, 2025

Another nice ride in Montana, by the numbers 60 miles and 1500 feet of elevation, but with a big downhill section towards the end, which helped a lot. Very nice clear weather, sky completely cloudless for whole morning and most of the afternoon.

The downhill at mile 27 was awesome – a bit more than seven miles at 7% grade, we were really flying along. The last 25 was a little trying since we had headwinds, but they were fairly mild winds, so no big deal.

At mile 10 of the ride we stopped at a National Park visitor’s center devoted to the site of a battle between the Nez Perce tribe and the US military back in the 19th century – pretty sad chapter in US history. Spent most of an hour watching a movie and looking over the exhibits.

As with many other Native American tribes they signed a series of treaties with the US government, each one successively shrinking their territory, then finally they balked when the next US-proposed agreement would have reduced their land by 90% … at a certain point after that they were on the run (fleeing from a group of US troops who were trying to take their horses away and relocate them) … then in the Big Hole river valley they were attacked at 3AM by troops again while camped resulting in a huge massacre of women and children. Pretty painful stuff to learn about, but there was a lot of that going on back then.
