Day 33: Franklin NE to Arapahoe NE

Posted on May 22, 2025

Medium-intensity ride today, 60 miles, 2200 feet of elevation gain, heading mostly west and northwest up to Arapahoe, Nebraska. Winds were favorable, mild and out of the northeast for most of the time. The mood of our riders was good, I think everyone is looking forward to a nice weekend, and enjoying the weather, which has been awesome.

Nebraska definitely feels like a “big sky” country; there were a couple of points where we stopped on the ride and just soaked in the expanse: farmland (corn) from one horizon to another. Still definitely with a drier and scrubbier feel to the vegetation and the terrain.

Seen on the road. Unfamiliar with that steer breed…

A shot of the sky and horizon on the way, and then a panorama shot:

We had a really nice stop at mile 22 or so in the town of Alma, they had a place called the Dragonfly Cafe – I had a large coffee and a caramel pecan roll, which was superb. In fact it was so good I went back in and ordered another one. Also encountered in the cafe was a mom with two month old triplets (in their baby carriers, sleeping), super cute. Don’t see that very often.

In Arapahoe the lovely people running the local Methodist church agreed to let us stay there overnight (yay, no tent pitching), and even better offered to host a potluck dinner for us. Outstanding! I am feeling very pampered already…

Looking forward to sleeping indoors tonight, then a nice short (45 miles) ride to McCook NE where we will be having a day off.
