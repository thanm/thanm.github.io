Day 39: More Reflection on Gear, this time at the Two Thousand Mile Mark

Posted on May 28, 2025

Another post on gear:

Most of my major gear items still going strong (especially my bike), but in the last few weeks my sleeping pad seems to have developed a slow leak … I get it all inflated, go to sleep, and in about four hours it has lost most of its oomph. I am planning on replacing it when we get to Fort Collins, looking forward to that. It was about a five year old pad, so not too much of a surprise that it gave out.

I also managed to lose one of my earbuds (oops) and mangled one of the buckles on my tent by stepping on it … those are fairly easily solved problems however, and not really due to “wear and tear” per se.

It is interesting to think about that fact that we’re all using our camping gear so intensively – most people might use their tents and sleeping bags and such for maybe two, three weeks a year total at most (once you add it all up), whereas we’re doing almost four to to five times that much on this jaunt. Adding to that, we pack up + move + unpack our stuff every day, which I think adds even more stress to the mix.

As of today I still have not had any flat tires (hopefully I haven’t just jinxed myself by writing this), pretty amazing when you get right down to it. Some of my fellow riders have had a half dozen flats already at this point. Simona actually had simultaneous flats on both wheels during the ride today due to the evil goat’s head thorns.
