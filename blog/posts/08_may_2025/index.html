Day 19: Cloverdale IN to Casey IL

Posted on May 8, 2025

Long ride today, 72 miles with 2500 feet of elevation gain, but way less punishing than our last long ride – the winds were favorable and the roads were good. The first 30 miles in particular were very zoomy and pleasant.

At mile 35 we stopped in Terre Haute Indiana, home of ISU; my fellow rider Mike and I had a nice second breakfast at a diner in town.

A big chunk of the riding today was on SR 40, a two-lane highway with a very limited shoulder, so we had to ride carefully and keep a close eye on traffic.

We had a time zone switch, so we gained an hour, which is nice.

Casey turns out to be an interesting town – according to Simona there were a couple of factories here that supported a lot of the working folks, and around 12 years ago those factories all shut down (I assume moved to some other lower-wage locale). For a while this was a major economic crisis for the town, particularly for the shops and restaurants. Then a local businessman decided that he would build the “world’s largest wind chime” (imagine a set of wind chimes about the size of a tree) so as to bring in tourist visitors from I70. Lo and behold, his scheme worked, so he and others wound up building a bunch of other “worlds largest” objects (rocking chair, mailbox, pencil, etc) and as of today the town’s shops and businesses seem to be on at least a decent standing.

Today was supposed to be a laundry day (definitely needed) but when we arrived at the town laundromat we found it all boarded up – according to the lady in the ice cream shop, someone broke into the place a week or so ago and they shut it down and boarded it up while they decide how to fix it. Whaaa? Who would have guessed …

Big rainstorm a little while after we arrived, it blew in quickly and started dumping rain on us (at that point most folks had successfully pitched theirs tents), so we had another small “wait out the rain” episode starting around 2:45.
