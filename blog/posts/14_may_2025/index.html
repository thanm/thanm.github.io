Day 25: Boonville MO to Higginsville MO

Posted on May 14, 2025

Back to riding on pavement! Super zoomy ride today, we had a huge tailwind, which was lovely – getting wafted along as if you were riding an e-bike or something. The route itself was nice too, very slow rolling hills and lots of wide open spaces.

Selfie on the road:

The road in the background is pretty representative of what we had to work with today – surface ok, but with a nasty loose gravel shoulder, you definitely want to stay off that unless it is an absolute emergency.

We passed Arrow Rock, a spot where the Santa Fe trail intersects with the Lewis + Clark route, but there was not much to see there since the visitor’s center was closed.

We stopped around 1pm in the microscopic to town of Blackburn MO, where amazingly there was a small cafe that was open: Ed’s Burgers and Beer. Super shoestring operation, just one guy, Ed. He turned out to be a late 60’s early 70 dude with lots of grey hair and a friendly demeanor.

The ride today:

We are camping in a county fairground, lots of space and lots of nice level places to pitch tents. No showers however, drat! Everyone is super sweaty and gross, I would pay a lot of money to take a good shower at this point. I did run into a local cyclist person from the town who said that he might be able to call soeone from the parks department to get us into the swim center.
